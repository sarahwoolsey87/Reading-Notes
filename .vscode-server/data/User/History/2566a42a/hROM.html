Love it or hate it, there's no denying that cheese is one of the most ubiquitous and versatile foodstuffs in the Western world. Whether blue, red, soft, hard even fruity, it's difficult to think of a savoury meal that wouldn't be enhanced by some form of our dairy overlord.

Addicted to cheese? It's a gouda sign!

Whilst it may be the dieter's enemy, cheese has many benefits for our physical and mental wellbeing. Packed with calcium alongside Vitamins A, D, K and zinc, it is a significant contributer to bone health.

Shredded, Melted or Sliced: Which is the cheddar way to enjoy that grateness?

Cheese snobs might opine that cheese should be a meal on it's on, thinly sliced alongside palate cleansing crackers, bland grapes and an artisan chutney but this view is waning in popularity over the last decade. Social media videos have popularised the idea of raclette style dishes, baked mediterranean style combinations and even the acquired tasting liquid 'cheeze' favoured by some Americans.
